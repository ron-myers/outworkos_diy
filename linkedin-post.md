Your inbox is a to-do list you don't control.

Someone else decided what's on it. Someone else decided the order. And the most recent message sits at the top, regardless of whether it's the most important thing you could be doing right now.

I get 100-200 emails a day. I've tried Getting Things Done, Inbox Zero, the Eisenhower matrix. I go through cycles of feeling on top of it, only to watch it fill up again with no clear way to reorient around what actually matters.

So I built a system to fix it. Outwork OS is a personal operating system that triages my inbox, syncs my tasks, preps me for meetings, and drafts emails with the right context. 30+ commands, all powered by Claude Code.

I didn't write a single line of code. Claude built 100% of it. My job was understanding the problem, the experience I wanted, and the outcomes I expected.

It's open source and free. One command to get started:

npx create-outworkos

Or just open Claude Code and tell it to visit outworkos.me. It'll set itself up.
